~Sir Francis Drake (1540 – 1596) Sir Francis Drake was a feared privateer who struck terror into the Spanish, earning the nickname “The Dragon.” He was not a cruel man, avoiding murder or torture, but his success in raiding Spanish territories made him notorious. Drake was the first Englishman to sail around the globe, and he played a crucial role in defeating the Spanish Armada in 1588. In May 1572, Drake set sail with two ships and 73 men. After crossing the Atlantic, he used the Isle of Pines as a base to attack the Spanish. There, he assembled small attack ships, capable of navigating the shallow waters. These vessels were used in raids against the Spanish, notably at Nombre de Dios, a Spanish-controlled town in Panama. Drake and his men attacked the town at night, making as much noise as possible to create the illusion of a larger force. The Spanish, fearing the onslaught, fled, leaving behind a treasure trove. However, the crew feared the Spanish would return and abandoned the treasure. Undeterred, Drake turned his attention to the mule trains that crossed Panama carrying silver. A Spanish man warned the trains, and soon the treasure-laden mules were replaced with ones carrying only supplies. Nevertheless, Drake and his crew continued to target the mule trains. After several unsuccessful raids, they finally seized a mule train near Nombre de Dios. The Spanish soldiers fled, and Drake’s men captured a large treasure, though it was too heavy to carry all at once. Some of it was buried for later retrieval. Drake returned to England as a hero, and his exploits inspired many more journeys to the Americas. He continued his raids against the Spanish, adding to his fame. However, on his final voyage, Drake fell ill and passed away. He was buried near Nombre de Dios, marking the end of a remarkable career. Drake’s legacy as one of the most successful privateers in history remains strong. His achievements made him a national hero in England, and his daring raids against the Spanish solidified his place in history as one of the greatest maritime adventurers of the age.~ Quiz ?Why might Drake have avoided murder or torture during his raids? His bold raids continued with an attack on Maracaibo on the Gulf of Venezuela, where he faced off with a Spanish fleet. His daring actions earned him the reputation of being a brilliant strategist. His most famous conquest occurred in Panama, where he attacked the Castle of San Lorenzo and then led 1,400 men up the Chagres River to assault the Spanish. The Spanish forces fled, but Morgan and his men, exhausted from the journey, could not pursue them. After setting the city on fire, Morgan looted its treasures and sailed back to Port Royal, Jamaica. In April 1672, he was arrested for his attacks on the Spanish and sent to England. Though he faced charges, his fame saved him from conviction. In 1674, Morgan was knighted and made lieutenant governor of Jamaica. He spent the rest of his life indulging in tavern life rather than pursuing further military ambitions.
