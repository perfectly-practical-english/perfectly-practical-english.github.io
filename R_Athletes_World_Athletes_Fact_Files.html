Full Essay: Takeru Kobayashi Takeru Kobayashi: The Hot Dog Eating Champion Takeru Kobayashi is a famous Japanese competitive eater. He was born on March 15, 1978, in Japan. Kobayashi is best known for his incredible skills in eating hot dogs at the Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest in the USA. Kobayashi made history at Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest in 2001. The world record was 25 hot dogs in 12 minutes. But Kobayashi ate 50 hot dogs in the same time! This shocked everyone. He became famous because he was faster and ate more than anyone before him. One time, he ate so many hot dogs that he almost vomited. Kobayashi used a special method to eat. He would dunk the hot dogs in water to make them easier to swallow. This technique helped him eat so many hot dogs quickly. After this, other competitors started using his method. He also works out because he says exercising makes him hungrier. Over the years, Kobayashi won many eating contests and appeared on TV shows. In 2003, on a TV show called "Man vs. Beast," he competed against a 1,000-pound Alaskan brown bear. The bear ate 50 hot dogs in about 2 minutes, much faster than Kobayashi, who ate only 31. Kobayashi's biggest human rival was an American eater named Joey Chestnut, who later broke some of Kobayashi’s records. Takeru Kobayashi changed competitive eating. He showed people how to eat more and faster, and he made eating contests popular worldwide.
