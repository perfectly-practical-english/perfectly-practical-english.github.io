Final Comments Positive values are naturally expressed as positive behavior. Positive behavior is necessary for learning, so positive behavior is non-negotiable. It is therefore useful to reflect, from time to time, on what makes such behavior so important for success, and how we can acquire or maintain it. On the other hand, nobody is perfect, and everyone engages in negative or unproductive behavior sometimes. These behaviors are counterproductive for learning, so they cannot be allowed. Most of negative behaviors seem obvious, but because of human nature, we need to identify such behavior specifically so that we can recognize it and avoid it. It is therefore useful to reflect, from time to time, on what makes negative behavior so detrimental to success, and how we can avoid such behavior by focusing on doing what is right. And sometimes, it might be necessary to be reflective on how many seemingly innocent activities might actually be negative or unproductive, and rather choose to engage in positive, productive behavior that leads to success.
