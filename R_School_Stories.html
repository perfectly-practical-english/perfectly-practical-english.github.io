~Just a Joke On the first day of school, I walk into the classroom early, before most of the desks are taken. The room smells clean and there are colorful posters on the wall. I am eager to start the new year, and I want to do my best. I choose a seat near the front where I can see the board clearly and hear what the teacher says. School I s important to me because I want to be successful when I grow up. When the boy behind me reads my name on my notebook, he turns my name into something ugly . He sings my name quietly, but loud enough so that I can hear it, and a few of his friends can hear it. A few students look at him and smile. I thought he was a good guy. I feel confused and look at him as if to say, "Why are you doing this?" He grins and says, “Relax, it’s just a joke.” But I still feel embarrassed. My stomach feels sick, and the way I feel makes it hard to do my work. But I try to concentrate anyway and keep doing my work. I tell myself, "Stay focused." I show self-control and do not react. I keep working and turn my assignment in. A few weeks go by. The joke has become a habit. He says rude things in whispers, and between classes in the hallway when teachers are not around. Sometimes he bumps into me when we line up, or pushes up against too hard when he walks by. One time, my books fall on the floor, and he stepped over them instead of helping. What he does never looks serious. Sometimes he says he's sorry, but I do not believe him anymore. Sometimes I think I should complain, but I don't know if that would make him behave better or worse. Other students start joining in. Not all of them. Just enough. He repeats my name and looks around to see who is watching. Someone laughs too loudly. Someone else keeps their eyes on their phone. What surprises me is how many people seem to like him even more when he behaves badly. He is confident. He is loud. Teachers smile at him when he answers questions quickly. He is a smart guy, but he is not kind. In class, the noise around me makes it harder to concentrate. He makes comments and jokes while I am trying to hear the instructions. He taps his pen on the back of my chair again and again. The teacher says, “Everyone, settle down." I hope the teacher knows that I am a good student, and that I am not making always noise. But then I realize the teacher is not talking about me. I keep working steadily and complete the assignment. At home, I tell my parents what is happening while we are having dinner. My parents care about me and they listen without interrupting. They tell me that I have great value and great potential. They pray for me and encourage me to never give up. They pray for me and hug me, and they tell me to always be open and honest with teachers, school friends and even the naughty student himself about what is right and wrong. The naughty guy is the bad guy. If I do what is right, I'm a good guy. A hero, in a small way. One afternoon, during class, he says my name again. Louder than before. I look right into his eyes. I don't shout, but I say “Be quiet. It’s not funny. I am trying to do my work." The room becomes quiet. He shrugs and smiles in a mean way. “I was joking.” “I wasn’t joking," I say. I just stand there. The teacher looks up. She walks over. I thought I would get into trouble, but she quickly understands the situation. She asks questions and listens to the answers. Things do not magically change after that. He is still in my class, and his behavior is still quite rude sometimes. I notice that he treats many people this way. But fortunately, I have changed. If someone treats me badly, I call them out on it. And now I have courage to speak up when someone treats other people badly. That makes me feel good, because I know I can be strong and helpful.
